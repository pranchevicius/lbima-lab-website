About Us

The LBIMA Lab conducts research at the interface of molecular microbiology and applied biotechnology. Our studies focus on clinically relevant bacteria, exploring their molecular characteristics and adaptive strategies to understand microbial behavior and identify potential biotechnological applications.

Research Focus

The LBIMA Lab focuses on molecular microbiology and bacterial pathogenesis, with an emphasis on clinically relevant Gram-negative bacteria. We study their molecular characteristics and adaptive responses under antimicrobial challenge. Our work combines genomic analyses of resistance determinants with immuno-informatic approaches for antigen discovery. We also develop recombinant vaccine candidates and evaluate their immunogenicity and protective potential in vivo. We aim to generate insights applicable to biotechnology and infectious disease control.
