LBIMA Lab – Research in microbial interactions, antimicrobial resistance, and biotechnological applications

About Us

The LBIMA Lab conducts innovative research in molecular microbiology, antimicrobial resistance mechanisms, microbial ecology, glycobiology, and biotechnological applications aimed at developing new diagnostic methods, therapeutic strategies, and vaccines.

Coordinator: Maria Cristina da Silva Pranchevicius – ORCID: 0000‑0002‑0416‑7401. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Our Research Focus

  • Antimicrobial resistance: molecular profiling of resistant pathogens and therapeutic approaches.
  • Microbial interactions: predation, competition, and stress responses among bacterial communities.
  • Biotechnology and vaccine design: using proteomics and immunoinformatics for next‑generation therapeutics.
  • Glycobiology and host–pathogen dynamics.
